Mr. Anderson, survival skills AM207. A preview what is coming

The story begins in 1981. Mr Anderson was traveling on a twin engine for the richness of South America. His brother, Jeffrey, has made a lot of money finding gold on the Andean mountains. Jeffrey was the adventurous type, he dropped out of high school and followed a friend who was going down to South America. He returned back in 10 years a very rich man. Mr Anderson, stayed at school and then went to one of the most prestigious universities in North America, Harvard, where he studied computational science under the famous Prof Provlief. These studies never got Mr Anderson any money, fame or even popularity. He was a loner, shy and very uncomfortable with people he did not know. What nowadays people may may call a nerd, back then people called them peculiar.

After graduating from Harvard, he took odd jobs at different place in Boston but none of them satisfied his curiosity or his intellect.

When he heard that the mining companies in the desert of Atacama were looking for bright young people to uncover the riches of nitrite, and since he had a very good understanding of engineering and mathematics he decided to take the risk and go down there.

After a long trip to Lima, Peru, he boarded a twin engine that would take him to San Pedro in the middle of the desert, the driest place on earth; even lizards dried out here. Half way through the trip they were crossing a high pass of the Andes and over the desert. He could see down from his window the infinite desert.

It was then that he realized that one of the engines was on fire. He informed the pilot, who told him not to worry and they could continue on the other engine. But all of the sudden the other engine stopped operating. The pilot was quick to jump out of the plane with the only one chute that was available. Anderson was ready to die, sat on the seat and simply came to the conclusion that life was all about luck. He remembered the words his professor telling him about randomness.


The plane went down very fast and luckily landed on the soft sand. Mr. Anderson survived despite the odds. "There is always that small probability and if you do not simulate things well, you will miss those outliers", he remembered his wise professor telling him. He was so right, he thought.

He got out of the plane, dusted himself off, happily concluded that nothing was broken and he survived a plane crash, he started examining the surroundings. There was absolutely nothing around. No water no food. I will not survive, he thought. The voice of his professor was in his head though. "There is always that small little probability, the world is ergodic and if you search hard and smart enough you can get everywhere. " Wished now that he knew what ergodic meant.

He took the GPS device (an old version of GPS) and started walking west hoping he could get to the coast. He knew the coast was hundred of kilometers away but he tried to use any information he had to his benefit. He also took with him all medical supplies and whatever else he could get.

After 3 hours of walking, exhausted, thirsty and about to collapse he saw something like a water. He ran and indeed, there was a little pond.



'How lucky I am', he thought. He was ready to drink when he realized that there was a sign "Poison, do not drink". He looked into the medical supplies for he remembered there was something resembling an antidote for poisonous water.

Got it out and read the instructions. One drop per cubic meter of water. Do not use more. Excess use can have severe side effects, such as blindness. Mr. Anderson has to calculate the volume of the water. Luckily the pond seem to have a constant depth of about the length of his palm, which he knew it was 30cm. He knew he had to calculate the volume of the water in the pond. How though? Now he remembers what he learned back in Harvard under professor Provlief. He formed a square on the sand using his body as a measuring stick, throw stones in the air and by measuring the number of stones that landed in the water vs the total number of stones he was able to calculate the area of the pond and therefore the volume of the water.

\(A_{pond} = \frac{N_{in}}{N_{total}} A\)

He continued his westward direction, however all of sudden a sand storm started and visibility became very limited; he could not see anything now and had hard time breathing.

He knew if he could go at the highest point he would have a much better chance in surviving. But without seeing the task of finding the highest point was impossible. Luckily again, his old professor has taught him how to find the maximum in a parameter space, which seemed to be perfectly appropriate for his situation. Remembering his professor's explanation, he stepped in a random direction and feeling if he went up or down. If he stepped downwards he reversed his position and tried again, otherwise he continued from the new position. Also he remembered from his lectures that every now and then he should be accepting bad positions, or going downwards in order to avoid local maxima. Following this procedure, three hours later he was able to be at the high ground and saved his life once more.

He stayed at the high ground for a while until the sand storm passed. Unfortunately, now it was cloudy and he could not see the sun and during the sand storm he lost his sense of directionality. He had the old GPS but also found a pedometer that could provide him with estimates of his speed and with some basic astronomy he knew he could approximately find the direction. Started walking westward he faced the dilemma: should he be trusting the old GPS or should he be trusting the pedometer and his estimations? All of sudden he remembered Prof. Provlief lectures on Kalman filters. He remembered that this was a way to find out which one to believe more. Using the Kalman's gain he was able to maintain course and in few hours of walking he saw in the far lights of a little village.

\(\hat{X}_k = K_k \, Z_k +(1-K_k) \hat{X}_{k-1}\)

I am saved he thought. He started running towards the lights but when he arrived closer, he realized that this was not civilization as he was hoping for. This was a small dwelling out in the country with few people sitting around the fire playing a game. They looked like outlaws, certainly not to be trusted. He had no other choice but to approach them. He was hungry, tired, had not slept or had water for few days...

Mr Anderson asked for water but the characters of the camp told him that in the desert nobody gives you water; you have to earn it. Next they invited him to join them in a game of dudo, a game similar to poker but played with dice. Mr Anderson knew nothing of the game but quickly realized that it is all based on probability theory. After few rounds, he realized that all the players were calculating probabilities where the game is based on commutative probability and extreme value distributions. Thanks to a little project he did with Prof. Provlief he had a small computer program using Markov Chain Monte Carlo to calculate complicated integrals of probability distributions. Luckily he had on his Texas Instrument calculator the program that he used for the project. Quickly he modified the code and soon after he started to win round after round. Winner of the game, he left the camp with water, food and a horse, feeling good about himself and all the time he spent with Prof. Provleif. Again it saved his life.

On the horse now, he continued his journey trying to get out of the desert and still determined to make a lot of money before returning home. After a day of riding he came upon a little mining village. He was so happy to eventually be in a place he could take a hot meal and a bath. Rest and continue on his quest to become rich. He checked into a local hotel and went for dinner at a local bar called the Empanadillas Caliente. Sat down on the bar next to a big strong man, Mr. Jimenez was his name. He asked him what he was doing and he told him that he was a mining engineer looking for iodine. Apparently there was a lot of demand because of the expanding business of photography and iodine was used in films. Mr Jimenez was looking to find ways to extract iodine from nitrate only to be failing. He explained the problem as mixed pools of nitrate and iodine but nobody knew how to find where the pools were. They only had sonars that they used to find the core but could not distinguish between the two. After couple of piscos, he again remembered the lectures of Prof. Provlief about expectation maximization and how one can find out the values of things not directly observed but can be deduced. He continued conversing with Mr. Jimenez and quickly realized that the methods he learned can be applied here. This led to a vivid discussion between two technically savvy people while consuming a lot of piscos. By the morning they have figured out the likelihood function and on his little calculator he was able to run the code and estimate the amount of iodine vs nitrate on different possible reservoirs.

In the next few months they worked intensively to perfect the method and started a new company in the exploration of iodine. Their new company started making money and soon Mr. Anderson had enough money to feel good about things. His dream was coming together - eventually.

Unfortunately his relation with Mr. Jimenez went bad after the first few months. It turned out that Mr Jimenez was not so honest with his dealings and he made Mr. Anderson sign a bad contract. Mr Anderson did this without paying attention assuming that Mr. Jimenez was his friend. At the end he landed up with only a dozen thousand dollars and out of the company. Totally disappointed but at least with a little cash he took the first plane out of South America to London.

First thing he hit the local pub. There he met Mr. Muray who was working at a financial firm in equity trading. Mr. Anderson and Mr. Muray become friends over a warm beer at the pub. Mr Muray was talking about how trading was done and again he connected back to Prof. Provlief lectures on Hidden Markov Models and how to simulate sequential events of hidden variables. Quickly he pulled his little computer and started calculating. Long story short he is now a multimillioner trading on wall street.
